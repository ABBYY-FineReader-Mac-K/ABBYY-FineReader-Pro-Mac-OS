## Getting the Best Recognition Results

<a href="#">ABBYY FineReader Mac OS</a> recognition is highly accurate, and a few practices push
results even higher:

**Scan quality**: Higher-resolution scans recognize more accurately. A clean 300 DPI scan gives
the engine the detail it needs for reliable results.

**Correct language**: Confirming the document language — or enabling the right combination for
multilingual documents — ensures the engine applies the correct dictionaries and character sets.

**Straight pages**: Deskewing crooked scans before recognition improves accuracy, and FineReader
can correct skew automatically as part of processing.

**Review after recognition**: For critical documents, a quick review of the recognized text
catches the rare misread character before the document is finalized.

## Organizing a Paperless Workflow

Adopting <a href="#">ABBYY FineReader Pro Mac OS</a> is often the first step toward a genuinely
paperless setup, and a consistent workflow makes the transition stick:

**Standardize your scans**: Scanning at a consistent resolution and format keeps recognition
quality predictable and your archive uniform. A 300 DPI scan to PDF is a reliable baseline for
most documents.

**Recognize on intake**: Running OCR the moment a document enters your system — rather than
letting scans pile up as images — means everything in your archive is searchable from day one,
with no backlog to process later.

**Name and file consistently**: Because recognized documents are searchable, a light naming
convention plus full-text search makes retrieval fast. You no longer depend on remembering exactly
where a file lives; you can search its contents.

**Keep originals until verified**: For important documents, retaining the physical or original
scanned copy until the recognized version is checked provides a safety net during the transition.

Over time, this routine turns <a href="#">ABBYY FineReader Mac</a> into the front door of a
searchable digital archive where every document — no matter how it arrived — is editable and
findable.
